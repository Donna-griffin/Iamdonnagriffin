About me

I’m mostly a single player game kind of person, like RPG or action adventure or stealth. As a result, I’m somewhat sheltered from the notoriously toxic online gaming community. But that's not to say I don't interact with other games on forums and social media. And there is one type of gamer that I particularly despise: The so-called “hardcore gamer". Now before we go any further, I want to make something clear: I'm a woman. And at age 38, I'm an “elderly woman” in terms of video gamers. I'm the type of woman who dresses up (in gamer standard) and wears make up. I look like your regular “middle management" corporate drones. I have an equal amount of dress shirts and novelty T-shirts with clever video game/pop culture references like “Jesus Saves, and takes half damage". In other words, I'm not the type you would traditionally associate with gamer. Except, I've been playing games for over two decades, and have been working in the video game industry for nearly a decade. I have all the major consoles, and the most valuable possession I have (other than my condo), is my Steam account. While I don't have 1000 hours logged for any online multiplayer game (as I've said, I'm mostly a single player gamer), I do spend significant amount of my free time playing games. And consider that I also work on video games, you can say majority of my time is spent on video games one way or another. So by the most strict “hardcore gamer" standard, I'm considered a hardcore gamer. And yet I hate the title. I never self-proclaimed to be a hardcore gamer. When people ask, I usually say, I play video games. I’m passionate about video games. I don't want people to think of me as one of those people, the “proud hardcore gamers", who: Confuse their passion about the game with entitlement of the genre. Believe they alone have the power to define good/bad games. Believe they alone have the power to define what kind of people are considered gamers, and more importantly, who don't deserve be part of the fandom. Believe they're entitled for the developers to cater towards their likes and dislikes because they're passionate about a game. In other words, those who consider themselves to be “hardcore gamer" with a smirk on their face, also do the most Geek gatekeeping. And it gets worse if the said hardcore gamer is a woman. Oh my goodness… This is the type I hate the most. More than the MRA people, more than the trolls, more than the Gamer Gate mobs… I particularly despise a certain group of female gamers who believe a set of behavior rules for other female gamers.
